# HCM ADR Template

## *One sentence summary e.g “How we would like to propose designs.”*

# Executive Summary

A quick summary of the decision.

# What

Give here a short summary of what decision this document is trying to reach and what components it is touching.   
*For example: This decision doc is proposing an approach for a hybrid cloud application look and feel*

# Why

Put here a motivation behind the change proposed by this decision document, give context. Explain why it is important that a decision gets made.  
*For example: It’s important to clearly explain the reasons behind certain design decisions in order to have a consensus between team members, as well as external stakeholders. Such a design document can also be used as a reference and for knowledge-sharing purposes. That’s why we are proposing a consistent style of the design document that will be used for future designs.*

Additionally lay out the present circumstances that require this change ( the following section then can be used to explain the problems arising, shortfalls and challenges in the present situation ).

## Goals

Goals for the solution as proposed in HOW:

* Allow easy collaboration and decision making on design ideas.  
* Have a consistent design style that is readable and understandable.  
* Have a design style that is concise and covers all the essential information.

## Non-Goals

* Move old designs to the new format.  
* Not doing X,Y,Z

  # How

Explain the full overview of the proposed solution. Some guidelines:

* Make it concise and **simple**; put diagrams; be concrete, avoid using “really”, “amazing” and “great” (:   
* How will you test and verify?  
* How you will migrate users, without downtime. How do we solve incompatibilities?  
* Who will run the solution? At a high level, what needs to be true for the proposal to be operational?  
* What open questions are left? (“Known unknowns”)

## Alternatives

The section stating potential alternatives. Highlight the objections the reader should have towards your proposal as they read it. Tell them why you still think you should take this path \[[ref](https://twitter.com/whereistanya/status/1353853753439490049)\]

1. This is why not solution Z…  
2. Explain, if needed, the impact of not doing the work or not taking the decision being requested.

## Risks

What is the risk context, and what mitigation might look like for \[ operability, branding, monetary, people, ambiguity, uncertainty \] :

* Execution risk \- reasons we may fail to execute this as proposed, risk of not getting this done within some timeline, etc.  
* Adding risk to service, product, business ?  
* Removing risk in service, product, business ?  
* Customer risk \- does this change behavior in existing functionality or restrict how customers can use the platform / service?  
* Operational risk \- what risk does this solution bring in regards to operational cognitive load, toil, critical dependencies and failure modes against efficiency?
